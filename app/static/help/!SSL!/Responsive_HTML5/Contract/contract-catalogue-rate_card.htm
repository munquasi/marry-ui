Break Management

Break management tab shows whether the customer is charged for the break given to the G4S employees or not. It also shows whether the G4S employees are paid for those breaks or not. There is also a feature of  limiting the time period of the break using the tabs Break Length and Break Entitled Over.

 

Breaks can be of following four types :

  • Billed to the Client but not paid to employee,

  • Billed to client and paid to employee

  • Not Billed to Client and not paid to Employee

  • Not Billed to Client and paid to employee

  • By default the break is Billed to client and paid to employee

 

 

To add a break management:

 

  1. In the Break Management tab select any one or both among the Paid or Billed check box. By default both check boxes is selected.

 

 

When Break is Paid and Billed, Break Length and Break Entitled Over fields are not required.

 

  1. Uncheck any one or both according to the requirement.

 

Uncheck Paid if the break is paid to employee but Not Billed to the Client.

 

 

Uncheck Billed if the break is Billed to the Client but not paid to employee.

 

 

Uncheck both if the break is Neither Billed to the client nor paid to the Employee

 

 

When break is Not payable and/or not billable then user is able to enter Break Length (in minutes). Default value for Break Length will be the value which will be set in country module.

 

  1. Enter the Break Length in minutes (if required to change).

 

The Break Length field will be added with Break Entitled Over (in hours) field which is fetched from the Country Module. (it will be blank if nothing is mentioned in the country module regarding break entitled Over). Break Length can't be less than that defined at country level.

 

  1. Enter the Break Entitled Over in hours (if required to change).

For billing the customer on the basis of the man-hours given by the employees of G4S, separate Rate Card lines are made at every contract level.

Based on these Rate Card lines, special rate is assigned for separate time durations.

 

Billing Rate Card lines will generate only when the user makes the status of Location from  Draft  to Active.

 

There are four types of billing from Rate Card Tab:

●      Arrears Variable billing

●      Arrears Fixed billing

●      Advance Variable billing

●      Advance Fixed billing

Billing for all four are described below.

Arrears Variable Billing

Arrears variable billing is done on the basis of actual data where an employee is present on a shift for a post. The user can register / edit the employee’s attendance, overtime, early leaving and other activities by clicking on the Edit Actuals option present on the Time & Attendance drop down list.

 

Please refer Edit Check In/ Check Out  to know how to edit check in/ check out

 

Once the check in/check out is done under the Edit Actual Tab, the Proof of Presence (POP) is made for the respective employee. POP is a technical term for attendance. It is further assigned a presence ID for reference. This Presence ID is used for making the Billing line. Once the billing lines are made, the finance department starts the billing procedure.

Annual Contracted Value (ACV)

ACV tab provides a history of all the Annual Contracted values (ACV) with the Start dates and End Dates. After clicking on Save in the edit check in / check out, the Annual Contracted Value (ACV) is created for the saved data.

 

On the basis of ACV, the billing is done for the following.

●      Arrear Fixed Billing

●      Advance Variable Billing

●      Advance Fixed Billing

 

Different billing lines are created in all the above three Billings.

For every post there will be a different billing cycle in the Bill Plan.

 

The ACV can be viewed by going to the post tab under the Location column of a Work Order.
